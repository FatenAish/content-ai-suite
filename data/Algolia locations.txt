Q1: When do we need to add Algolia locations to articles?
Short Answer:
 After the article is published.
Details:
 Once an article goes live, the content reviewer must ensure the correct Algolia tag is added and logged in the tracking sheet for review history and consistency.
________________


Q2: Which articles require Algolia tagging?
Short Answer:
 Any article linked to a specific location.
Details:
 If the article focuses on a community, building, city, or region, it requires a location tag. UAE-wide, conceptual, and discussion-based content does not require tagging.
________________


Q3: Who is responsible for adding Algolia locations?
Short Answer:
 Writers add tags, reviewers verify them.
Details:
 Writers apply Algolia tags during content creation. During weekly review, the checker verifies accuracy and logs tag status. If missing or incorrect, the reviewer requests correction.
________________


Q4: How often should Algolia locations be reviewed?
Short Answer:
 Weekly.
Details:
 A dedicated weekly review ensures recently published and updated content is tagged correctly and reflects tracking accuracy.
________________


Q5: Where do we log Algolia tagging status?
Short Answer:
 In the Algolia tracking Google Sheet.
Details:
 All actions must be recorded, including published date, URL, post ID, tag applied, exemption status, and reviewer comments.
________________


Q6: What if an article has no applicable location?
Short Answer:
 Mark it as exempt.
Details:
 If content applies to the entire UAE or has no geographic focus, record it as "No Location Required" to avoid repeating checks.
________________


Q7: What if the correct location does not exist in the Algolia list?
Short Answer:
 Request it to be added.
Details:
 Notify the platform owner or responsible party to create the new Algolia entry. Meanwhile, log the article as pending and follow up once available.
________________


Q8: What if a writer forgets to add the location tag?
Short Answer:
 Send it back for correction.
Details:
 Notify the writer and log the event. Repeat offenders should be flagged to management for workflow retraining.
________________


Q9: Do updated or republished articles need tagging checks?
Short Answer:
 Yes, if the content relevance changed.
Details:
 Any refreshed or updated article should be reviewed to confirm the current tag is correct. Changes must be logged.
________________


Q10: How do I choose which location to use?
Short Answer:
 Select the main focus location.
Details:
 Choose the most specific location relevant to the content topic (community → building → city → region). Avoid tagging every location mentioned.
________________


Q11: What if an article references multiple locations?
Short Answer:
 Tag only the primary one.
Details:
 Only assign a single location unless the article format is specifically a comparison or multi-region guide.
________________


Q12: Should UAE-wide articles be tagged?
Short Answer:
 No.
Details:
 Articles that apply nationally or broadly across the UAE do not require Algolia tags. They must still be logged as exempt.
________________


Q13: Should discussion-style articles be tagged?
Short Answer:
 No.
Details:
 If the content isn't tied to a specific geographic area, tagging is not needed. Still record exemption.
________________


Q14: What if I’m unsure which location applies?
Short Answer:
 Ask the content lead.
Details:
 If unclear, pause tagging, log the article, and consult the assigned content owner or team lead for verification.
________________


Q15: What happens when tagging is completely missed?
Short Answer:
 It must be corrected.
Details:
 Add the missing tag during review and document the update. Persistent oversight should be escalated.
________________


Q16: Should I notify someone when I correct a missed tag?
Short Answer:
 Yes, if repeated.
Details:
 Occasional errors are corrected quietly, but repeated issues should be flagged with evidence to a manager for process adjustment.
________________


Q17: Why do we maintain a tracking sheet?
Short Answer:
 For visibility and quality control.
Details:
 It prevents duplicate checks, enables auditing, tracks missed or pending items, and ensures accountability across the content workflow.
________________


Q18: Can I change or delete previous tracking entries?
Short Answer:
 No.
Details:
 The tracking sheet maintains a history of actions. If updates are required, add a new entry or update status without deleting original logs.
________________


Q19: Do old articles need tagging checks?
Short Answer:
 Yes, if they fall under location-based topics.
Details:
 Legacy relevant articles must be reviewed and updated in cycles. Progress should be documented until coverage is complete.
________________


Q20: What if the WordPress location tag is incorrect?
Short Answer:
 Fix it.
Details:
 Correct the tag in WordPress and update the tracking sheet. If tagging errors repeat for the same content source, escalate.
